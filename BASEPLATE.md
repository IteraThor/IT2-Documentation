## 1. General Overview: The IT2 Baseplate

While the IT2 system can be mounted directly to the wall, the Baseplate offers several key advantages:
*   **Structural Rigidity:** The 4-part design provides superior stability compared to 7-part alternatives. This increased stiffness minimizes bending and flexing, ensuring your camera arms remain in their exact positions.
*   **Minimal Drilling:** Only 3 holes are required in your wall instead of 6+.
*   **Perfect Alignment:** The baseplate ensures that your camera arms are perfectly centered and leveled relative to the bullseye.
*   **Sound Dampening:** Integrated circular recesses for dedicated TPU dampeners to reduce noise transmission.
*   **Stand Compatibility:** Native compatibility with the **Winmau Xtreme 2** mobile dartboard stand by default and without any modifications.

## 3. General Assembly: Baseplate & System

### 3.1 Step 1: Installing Heat Inserts

> 💡 **Important:** While a "Self-Tapping" version of the baseplate is available, this only applies to the M4 mounting points. The **M6 heat inserts** for the Rota Locks are **always mandatory** for all versions, as these parts are adjusted frequently and require the durability of a metal thread to prevent wear.

If you are using the **Heat Insert version**, melt the M4 inserts into the designated holes at the ends of the three mounting arms. 

![heat_insert_arm](images/05_baseplate/04_heat_insert_arm.png)
![heat_insert_center](images/05_baseplate/05_heat_insert_center.png)

> 💡 **M4 Heat Insert Guide for Dartboard Mounting:**
> Standard Wall Mount: Use **2 inserts as shown in the picture**.
> Portable Stand or Low Ceiling: Use the **other two unused holes** (or all four) per arm.
> Addons: Additional inserts may be required for [Addons](#5-pro-tips-advanced-features--addons).

> 
> ⚠️ **Orientation:** The photos show the **standard alignment**. If you are building for a dart stand or low ceiling, ensure you orient the arms correctly using the alternative mounting holes.

### 3.2 Step 2: Joining the Segments
The three mounting arms are joined to the center point using a simple **face-to-face screw connection**. 
1. Align the mounting arms with the center point.
2. Secure each arm using the provided screw points.

### 3.3 Step 3: Attaching the IT2 System
It is highly recommended to attach your assembled IT2 scoring system to the baseplate before mounting it to the wall or stand. However, you can also perform this step afterwards if preferred.
1.  Place your assembled IT2 system (the three camera arms connected to the ring) onto the baseplate.
2.  Align the legs of the camera arms with the mounting points on the baseplate arms.
3.  Secure the system using **three M4 screws**.

## 4. Final Step: Installation & Mounting

The IT2 Baseplate can be mounted either directly to a wall or onto a mobile dartboard stand. Choose the section below that fits your setup.

### 4.1 Mounting Instructions: Wall Mounting (Option A)
Installing the IT2 Baseplate to the wall is simple and precise. 

1.  **Find the Bullseye:** Measure and mark the official bullseye height (**1.73m**) on your wall.
2.  **Positioning:** Align the center hole of the baseplate with your mark. If possible, you can already use a screw for the center point - this allows you to freely rotate the baseplate for perfect alignment.
3.  **Alignment:** Use the "LR" indicators on the arms to position one of the arms in the direction of either the **11 segment** (Left) or the **9 segment** (Right). 
4.  **Leveling:** Finally, use a spirit level on the outer edge to level the baseplate.
5.  **Securing:** Secure the assembly using the remaining wall screws through the holes shown in the image below.

![baseplate_mounting_steps](images/05_baseplate/08_mounting_steps.png)

> ⚠️ **Important:** The baseplate adds **30mm of depth** to your setup. You **MUST** move your oche (throw line) back by 30mm to maintain official tournament distances.

### 4.2 Mounting Instructions: Dartboard Stand (Option B)
This method allows for a fully mobile setup. The IT2 Baseplate features a mounting pattern explicitly designed to fit the **Winmau Xtreme Dartboard Stand 2** without modifications.

1.  **Alignment:** Align the baseplate mounting holes with the brackets of your stand.
2.  **Securing:** Use the hardware provided with your stand or M6 screws to secure the baseplate.
3.  **Support (Optional):** If your setup feels top-heavy, we recommend using the additional support addons provided in the print profile (requires 2 extra M6 heat inserts).

## 5. Pro Tips: Advanced Features & Addons

The IT2 Baseplate system supports several modular addons to extend its functionality:

### 5.1 Noise Dampening (Optional Addon)
The back of the baseplate segments features **circular threaded recesses** designed for 3D-printed TPU dampeners. These inserts do not make the scoring system quieter for the player, but they effectively prevent vibrations and noise from being transmitted through the wall to other rooms.

#### Installation & Wall Mounting with Dampeners:
If you choose to use the TPU dampeners, the wall mounting process changes:

1.  **Prepare the Baseplate:** Use a screwdriver and a hammer (or a similar tool) to punch out the pre-perforated holes in the center of the baseplate recesses.
2.  **Insert Dampeners:** Screw the 3D-printed TPU dampeners into the threaded recesses.
3.  **Mounting:** Use the holes located in the center of the TPU dampeners to secure the entire assembly to the wall.

> ⚠️ **Important:** Do not over-tighten the screws. The TPU should be held securely but must not be crushed or overly compressed, as this would negate the dampening effect.

### 5.2 RGB LED Ring Expansion & Camera Shroud (Work in Progress)
An upcoming multipurpose expansion designed for both ambient lighting and scoring reliability:
*   **RGB LED Support:** Allows for mounting additional RGB LED strips around the perimeter of the light ring for custom visual effects.
*   **Detection Shroud:** The expansion also acts as a "shroud" or collar that encloses the system. This prevents the cameras from seeing background movements, which significantly improves dart detection.
*   **💡 Recommendation:** This shroud is highly recommended for **portable stand setups**, where a static background cannot always be guaranteed.

### 5.3 Upcoming Features (Work in Progress)
The following features are currently in development:
*   **Integrated Raspberry Pi Mount:** Native support for mounting a Raspberry Pi directly to the baseplate, creating a fully self-contained scoring unit.
*   **Expanded Stand Support:** We are currently evaluating support for additional dartboard stands (e.g., KOTO or budget alternatives) to be released in future updates.
